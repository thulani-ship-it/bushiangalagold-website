The Location

Where the Kakamega County mine is located

Kakamega County sits in Western Kenya, on a gold-bearing geological formation that extends through the Lake Victoria region. Gold extraction in this area is long-standing and well-documented. The Bushiangala site is within that belt.

The Reserve

An independently assessed deposit

The Kakamega County deposit has been independently assessed at a verified value of $5.28 billion, based on an ore body of 1.27 million ounces at an average grade of 11.43 grams per tonne. These are not projections or estimates. They come from an independent geological survey of this specific site.

For buyers who need to account for provenance at the compliance stage, those figures are significant. They confirm not just that gold exists here, but that it exists in a quantity and concentration that supports a commercial, long-term operation.

The Product

What this operation produces

Gold bars, gold nuggets, and gold dust are all produced from this site. Custom bar orders and large-volume bulk purchases are handled directly. Everything available through Bushiangala Gold Extractives was extracted here, at this location.

There is no secondary sourcing. Gold from other origins is not blended into orders. When a buyer receives an assay report confirming purity and origin, that report refers to gold from the Kakamega County operation. That is the point of working directly with the mine.

Government Credentials

The credentials tied to this site

Three credentials cover the Bushiangala operation at Kakamega County. A Mineral Trading Licence from the State Department of Mining in Kenya. A Mineral Export Permit from the Kenyan Ministry of Mining. And a NEMA Environmental Certificate from the National Environment Management Authority.

These are not held at company level and applied loosely across different activities. They are operational credentials tied to this specific site, this reserve, and this company. All three are individually verifiable and available on request.
